Lesson Park is a production-grade lesson aggregation platform for the Japanese market, enabling users to discover and compare sports, education, music, IT, and early-childhood lessons by category, prefecture, and location.

I designed a scalable, component-driven architecture focused on search performance, data normalization, and responsive UX. The platform supports high-volume listings, advanced filtering, and map-based discovery while maintaining accessibility-aware UI patterns and mobile-first design.

The system was structured to remain extensible beyond its no-code foundation, with clear separation between data models, workflows, and presentationâ€”allowing future migration to custom backends or external APIs without architectural rework.

##### Core Features

<ul>
  <li>Category-based lesson discovery (Sports, Music, Learning, IT, Early Education, etc.)</li>
  <li>Advanced search and filtering by prefecture and location</li>
  <li>Map-based nearby lesson discovery</li>
  <li>Scalable lesson listing and detail pages</li>
  <li>Responsive, mobile-first UI for parents and students</li>
  <li>Multilingual-ready content structure (Japanese-first)</li>
</ul>
